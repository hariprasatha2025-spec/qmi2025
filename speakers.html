Speakers

Dr. Amit Kumar Chauhan

Senior Research Associate, QNu Labs Pvt. Ltd., Bangalore.

Amit Kumar Chauhan is a Senior Research Associate at QNu Labs Pvt. Ltd., Bangalore, specializing in post-quantum cryptography and quantum cryptographic solutions. He previously served as a Research Engineer in the Post-Quantum Cryptography group at the Centre for Development of Telematics (C-DOT), New Delhi. Amit holds a Ph.D. in Cryptography from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at IIT Ropar and an M.Tech. in Computer Science from IIIT Delhi. Amit's research spans post-quantum cryptography, cryptanalysis, and quantum computation. He is a life member of the Cryptology Research Society of India and his contributions include the development of secure cryptographic protocols poised for the post-quantum era, and he remains actively engaged in both academic and industrial research advancing quantum-ready cybersecurity.

Dr. Aswath Babu

Assistant Professor and Head of the Department of Arts, Science, and Design, IIT Dharwad.

Dr. Aswath Babu H is an Assistant Professor and Head of the Department of Arts, Science, and Design at the Indian Institute of Information Technology (IIIT) Dharwad. He earned his Ph.D. in Physics from IIT Kanpur in 2013, specializing in nonlinear dynamical phenomena in all-optical bistable systems. His research focuses on quantum information theory, quantum optics, and nonlinear optics, with applications in quantum computing and secure communication. He previously held postdoctoral positions at IIT Madras and Lund University, Sweden, and served as faculty at NIT Calicut before joining IIIT Dharwad.

Prof. P. Balasubramaniam

Senior Professor of Mathematics, Dean School of Sciences, The Gandhigram Rural Institute-Deemed to be University, Gandhigram, Tamil Nadu, India.

Dr. P. Balasubramaniam is a Senior Professor in the Department of Mathematics at The Gandhigram Rural Institute - Deemed to be University (Ministry of Education, Govt. of India), Gandhigram, Tamil Nadu. He worked as a visiting research professor during the years 2001 and 2005-2006 to promote research in control theory and neural networks at Pusan National University, Pusan, South Korea. Also, he worked as Visiting Professor at the Institute of Mathematical Sciences, University of Malaya, Malaysia, for six months from September 2011 to March 2012. In 2022, he obtained a D.Sc. Degree in mathematics at the Gandhigram Rural Institute. He has published over 344 research papers in various SCI journals with impact factors with Scopus H-index 51 and Google Scholar H-Index 57. He is a reviewer of many SCI journals and a member of the editorial board of a few Journals. He is an Editor-inChief of the journal Modern Instrumentation, Scientific Research Publishing Inc. (SCIRP), ISSN Print: 2165-9257, and Associate Editor of Neural Processing Letters. He received the Tamil Nadu Scientist Award (TANSA) for the discipline of Mathematical sciences instituted by the Tamil Nadu State Council for Science and Technology in 2005. He received the Tamil Nadu Senior Scientist award in 2018 from Science City, Govt. of Tamil Nadu. He received the MidCareer award from UGC, Govt. of India, New Delhi, in 2019. He has been recognized as one of the Top 2% scientists in the area of AI & Image Processing since 2020 by the survey of Stanford University, USA. His research interests include the areas of dynamical systems, image processing, soft computing, cryptography, neural networks, and quantum computing.

Ms. Janani A

Quantum Engineer, IBM Research, Bengaluru, India.

Janani Anantha is a quantum engineer at IBM Quantum with over three years of experience spanning research and software development in quantum computation and information. She holds a BS-MS degree from IISER Pune and is an INSPIRE SHE Merit Scholar recognized for her academic excellence in STEM fields. Janani specializes in quantum algorithms, quantum error mitigation, quantum correlations, quantum control, and quantum machine learning, working with tools like Qiskit, Braket, cuQuantum, and Pennylane.​ Her professional background includes quantum support engineering at IBM, research roles at BosonQ Psi and QpiAI, and projects on optimization problems using variational quantum algorithms.

Dr. Mostafizur Rahaman

Prof. Dr. Raveendran Paramesran

Honorary Professor, Dept. of Electrical Engineering, University Malaya, Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia.

Prof. Raveendran Paramesran received his B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees in Electrical Engineering from South Dakota State University, USA, and his Ph.D. from the University of Tokushima, Japan, in 1994. He is currently an Honorary Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering at the University of Malaya, Kuala Lumpur. His research interests include image and video analysis, orthogonal moments, EEG signal analysis, and non-invasive data modeling. He has published widely and holds an international patent for predicting blood glucose levels using a non-parametric model.

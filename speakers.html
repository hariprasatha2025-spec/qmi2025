Speakers

International Speakers

Prof. Dr. Raveendran Paramesran

Honorary Professor, Dept. of Electrical Engineering, University Malaya, Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia.

Prof. Raveendran Paramesran received his B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees in Electrical Engineering from South Dakota State University, USA, and his Ph.D. from the University of Tokushima, Japan, in 1994. He is currently an Honorary Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering at the University of Malaya, Kuala Lumpur. His research interests include image and video analysis, orthogonal moments, EEG signal analysis, and non-invasive data modeling. He has published widely and holds an international patent for predicting blood glucose levels using a non-parametric model.

Dr. Keerthana Jaganathan

Lecturer, School of Computer Science, Northumbria University Newcastle, UK

Dr. Keerthana Jaganathan is a Lecturer in the Department of Computer and Information Science at Northumbria University, United Kingdom, and a member of the Data Science and Artificial Intelligence Research Group. She earned her Ph.D. in Explainable Artificial Intelligence from Jeonbuk National University, Korea, and previously worked as a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Newcastle University. Her research focuses on applied explainable AI, neural networks, and machine learning for drug discovery and energy storage systems.

National Speakers

Dr. D. Saraswathi

Associate Professor, School of Computer Science Engineering, VIT Chennai, Tamil Nadu.

Dr. D. Saraswathi is an Associate Professor at Vellore Institute of Technology (VIT) Chennai, with academic credentials including a B.E., M.E., and PhD in computer science and engineering. She is a passionate machine learning professional and researcher associated with the Centre for Human Movement Analytics at VIT, specializing in artificial intelligence, machine learning, deep learning, medical image analysis, computer vision, and foundational quantum computing concepts. Dr. Saraswathi has expertise in predictive modeling, explainable AI, time-series analysis, generative AI, multi-modal signal fusion and technical frameworks such as TensorFlow, Keras, and Scikit-learn. She also actively engaged in research collaborations with clinical partners and has provided mentorship and training in AI/ML and computer vision, making substantial contributions to academic and professional communities.

Dr. T. Kalaiselvi

Associate professor & Head, Department of Computer Science and Applications, The Gandhigram Rural Institute - Deemed to be University, Tamilnadu, India

Dr. T. Kalaiselvi is an Associate Professor and Head of the Department of Computer Science and Applications at The Gandhigram Rural Institute - Deemed University, Tamil Nadu, India. She specializes in medical image processing, artificial intelligence, IoT, and robotics. Her expertise covers MRI brain image analysis to enhance computer-aided diagnosis, telemedicine, and teleradiology technologies. Her research also involves designing intelligent systems for healthcare applications and advancing tele-health solutions using computational techniques. She has led DST-sponsored projects, including research under the Young Scientist Scheme, and contributes actively to academic leadership and mentorship within her department.

Dr. Pranab Dutta

CEO & Co-founder, GQD Labs, Bengaluru.

Pranab Dutta is the CEO and Co-founder of GDQLabs Pvt. Ltd., Bengaluru, specializing in advanced quantum sensing and precision measurement technologies. He led the development of India’s first quantum gravimeter and has extensive experience in atomic clocks, magnetometers, and quantum communication systems. A former researcher at IISER Pune, he is highly skilled in system architecture, algorithm design, hardware integration, and field deployment.

Dr. Aswath Babu

Assistant Professor and Head of the Department of Arts, Science, and Design, IIT Dharwad.

Dr. Aswath Babu H is an Assistant Professor and Head of the Department of Arts, Science, and Design at the Indian Institute of Information Technology (IIIT) Dharwad. He earned his Ph.D. in Physics from IIT Kanpur in 2013, specializing in nonlinear dynamical phenomena in all-optical bistable systems. His research focuses on quantum information theory, quantum optics, and nonlinear optics, with applications in quantum computing and secure communication. He previously held postdoctoral positions at IIT Madras and Lund University, Sweden, and served as faculty at NIT Calicut before joining IIIT Dharwad.

Dr. Mostafizur Rahaman

Research Scientist, IBM Quantum, Bengaluru.

Dr. Mostafizur Rahaman is a Research Scientist at the IBM Quantum Division, IBM Research Lab, Bangalore, and a Visiting Scientist at the RIKEN Quantum Information Physics Theory Research Lab, Japan. He earned his Ph.D. in Quantum Signal Processing and Optimization from IIT Kharagpur. His research focuses on quantum simulation, Hamiltonian dynamics, and the development of scalable quantum algorithms for optimization and fault-tolerant quantum computation. His current work explores the intersection of quantum machine learning, chemistry, and algorithmic optimization, aiming to bridge theoretical advances with practical quantum technologies.

Dr. Amit Kumar Chauhan

Senior Research Associate, QNu Labs Pvt. Ltd., Bangalore.

Amit Kumar Chauhan is a Senior Research Associate at QNu Labs Pvt. Ltd., Bangalore, specializing in post-quantum cryptography and quantum cryptographic solutions. Prof. P. Balasubramaniam

Professor of Mathematics, Gandhigram Rural Institute - Deemed University, India.

P. Balasubramaniam is a distinguished professor and researcher in control theory, with over 28 years of experience in teaching and research at Gandhigram Rural Institute-Deemed to be University, India. He received his Ph.D. in control theory from Bharathiar University in 1994 and has held notable positions, such as Visiting Research Professor at Pusan National University in South Korea from 2001 to 2006. He specializes in neural networks, image processing, control theory, stochastic differential equations, soft computing, stability analysis, and cryptography.​ Dr. Balasubramaniam has authored over 280 research articles, with a h-index of 31 and an outstanding record of publication in high-impact journals. He is the recipient of the Tamil Nadu Scientist Award in mathematical sciences and serves as Editor-in-Chief of Modern Instrumentation (Scientific Research Publishing) and Associate Editor of Neural Processing Letters (Springer). Recognized for both academic leadership and research excellence, his work has significantly advanced the fields of control theory and neural networks.
